I am not a theologian. This is a simple attempt to explain the Gospel in its theological and historical context from a my modern perspective. I have 30 years experience of reading the Bible and failing every day at applying it. This is my attempt to write out some of those lessons that lead me to be absolutely certain that Jesus is who He says He is.

A Post-Modern Biblical Commentary

The Biblical Commentary for a Modern Audience

"He has shown you, O mortal, what is good. And what does the Lord require of you? To act justly and to love mercy and to walk humbly with your God.” ~Micah 6:8 NIV

Old Testament New Testament

Matthew presents Jesus as the promised Messiah and King of the Jews, emphasizing His teachings, miracles, and the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies. Written around 80-90 AD

Greek Commentary

Mark portrays Jesus as the suffering Servant and focuses on His actions more than His teachings, highlighting His ministry, death, and resurrection. Written around 65-70 AD

Greek Commentary

Luke writes to present Jesus as the Savior of all people, not just the Jews, and emphasizes His compassion, miracles, and the universal scope of the Gospel. Written around 80-90 AD

Commentary

John emphasizes Jesus as the divine Son of God and stresses the importance of faith in Him for eternal life. Written around 90-100 AD

Commentary

Acts chronicles the early days of the Christian church, focusing on the ministry of the apostles, the spread of the Gospel, and the work of the Holy Spirit. Written around 80-90 AD

Commentary

Romans provides a comprehensive presentation of the Gospel, emphasizing justification by faith and God’s plan for Jews and Gentiles. Written around 55-58 AD

Commentary

1 Corinthians addresses various issues and conflicts in the Corinthian church, offering practical guidance on Christian living and church unity. Written around 55 AD

Commentary

2 Corinthians is a personal and pastoral letter from Paul to the church in Corinth, defending his apostleship and urging reconciliation and perseverance. Written around 55-56 AD

Commentary

Galatians refutes the necessity of the Mosaic Law for salvation and emphasizes justification by faith in Christ alone. Written around 48-49 AD

Commentary

Ephesians focuses on the unity of believers in Christ and describes the spiritual blessings and responsibilities of those in the body of Christ. Written around 60-62 AD

Commentary

Philippians is a joyful letter from Paul to the church in Philippi, emphasizing Christian joy, unity, and the humility and exaltation of Christ. Written around 61 AD

Commentary

Colossians confronts false teachings and emphasizes the supremacy and sufficiency of Christ in all things. Written around 60-62 AD

Commentary

1 Thessalonians encourages and instructs the Thessalonian believers in their faith, hope, and love, especially concerning the return of Christ. Written around 51 AD

Commentary

2 Thessalonians addresses misunderstandings about the second coming of Christ and encourages believers to stand firm in their faith. Written around 51-52 AD

Commentary

1 Timothy provides pastoral guidance and instruction on church leadership, sound doctrine, and godly living. Written around 62-66 AD

Commentary

2 Timothy is a personal letter from Paul to Timothy, urging him to persevere in faith, guard the Gospel, and fulfill his ministry. Written around 67 AD

Commentary

Titus contains instructions for church leadership and emphasizes the importance of sound doctrine and good works. Written around 63-65 AD

Commentary

Philemon is a personal letter from Paul to Philemon, appealing for the forgiveness and acceptance of his runaway slave, Onesimus. Written around 60-62 AD

Commentary

Hebrews presents Jesus as the ultimate High Priest and emphasizes the superiority of His priesthood and the New Covenant. Written around 64-68 AD

Commentary

James provides practical guidance on Christian living, emphasizing faith, wisdom, and the importance of good works. Written around 45-49 AD

Commentary

1 Peter offers encouragement and instruction to suffering Christians, emphasizing hope, faith, and perseverance in Christ. Written around 60-64 AD

Commentary

2 Peter warns against false teachers and emphasizes the importance of knowledge, godliness, and anticipation of the day of the Lord. Written around 67-68 AD

Commentary

1 John focuses on the love and obedience of believers, addressing the assurance of salvation, fellowship with God, and the importance of discerning truth from error. Written around 90-95 AD

Commentary
